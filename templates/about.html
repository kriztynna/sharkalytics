About Sharkalytics
The basics

Sharkalytics started out as a project to quantify the investment activity on ABC's Shark Tank. Unsatisfied by existing episode recap options that often misreported deal terms or omitted mention of the outcomes entirely, I set out to create a trustworthy reference guide for the true Shark Tank fan.

The project has evolved to include aggregate data on each of the sharks, interactive visualizations, and crowdsourced data collection efforts.

My name is Cristina. I am the curator, developer, and owner of Sharkalytics. You can reach me at cristina@sharkalytics.com.

Don't forget to follow @sharkalytics for updates to our site content!

Help Sharkalytics get more smarts!

Sharkalytics started out as a simple project to improve the transparency behind Shark Tank outcomes. It's already gone a long way on its own to collect and organize the data from every company's pitch on the show. But what comes next?

Deals fall through. Funded companies can still shutter, even after some success. Every once in a while, an investment gets bought out for an even bigger sum. Meanwhile, many others that didn't get a shark onboard are still kicking.

Much of this information is out there on the web, but it's a lot for this one-woman show to track on her own. If you like Sharkalytics and feel inclined to give back, here are some of the dimensions we're looking to add to the database:

  • Which deals closed? Which fell through? Examples: Cousins Maine Lobster, How Do You Roll?
  • Do the shark investors still own their stake in the company? If not, who owns it now? Example: Groovebook
  • Is this company still in operation? Did it ever even launch? Examples: MisoMedia, Turbobaster

To contribute, just leave a comment on the relevant company's page, as shown in these examples above.
